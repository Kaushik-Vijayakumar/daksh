Manual Robotics

Event Description:

Haggard because of copious burdens? Ever felt the need to hire a replacement for your chores? Design your very own Robotic Bondsman and divulge your commands to draft out distinct configurations. Evolution is inevitable when technology progresses and new ideas emerge. So put on your thinking caps and be ready for the bot scavenge! Bring out the best from your third arm and prove its mettle. Let your bot speak technology in the best way possible!


Problem Statement:

Competing with others always gives us excitement. In this event we provide you an opportunity to prove your skills in creating a manually controlled robot and competing with other participants. You have to design a bot which is capable of moving and picking a ball underneath it as well as above it.


Mission:

  • Getting down a ramp which is at a height of 300mm at an inclination of 22.5 degrees to a pair of rails which is at a height of 100mm from the base.
  • Collecting the balls from the outer zone and putting them in the ball dropping zone.
  • Then the robot has two options, either it has to pass through a rectangular frame of 350mmX350mm and collect balls from the inner zone which is kept at a height of 150mm from the base and drop in the ball dropping zone (or) the robot can also steal balls from the opponent’s basket and put them into their ball dropping zone.


Arena Description:

  • The game arena has the dimensions of 4200mm X 3000mm which is divided into two equal halves for each team.
  • The arena has an inner ball zone and a pair of outer ball zones (one for each team).3. The inner zone has the dimensions of 1800mm X 1800mm.
  • The inner zone has the dimensions of 1800mm X 1800mm.
  • Starting point of the robot is on the top of the ramp which is at a height of 300mm from the base with an inclination of 22.5 degrees. The platform dimensions are 400mm X 400mm.
  • The ramp leads to a pair of rails which is at a height of 100mm from the ground. Distance between the rails: 160mm. Width of each rail: 120mm.
  • The robot can reach the ground from the rails through a small ramp of inclination 15 degrees.
  • Outer ball zone is placed in between the two rails. It has 7 balls arranged in a straight line.
  • Inner ball zone is an elevated common zone of height 150mm for both the teams. This zone has 15 balls arranged in a 4X4 square grid.
  • The balls will be placed on pipes of 25mm diameter and 10mm height. The pipes are fixed to the arena. There will be a gap of 150mm between each ball.
  • All the splitting shown in the arena are 10mm high wall.
  • The Outer zone has a Pass zone of dimensions 100mm X 100mm. This is the place where the first ball has to be dropped in order to open the gate so that the robot can proceed to ball dropping zone.
  • Ball dropping Zone: It is where all the balls collected are to be dropped irrespective of the zone. The dimensions of the zone are 300mm X 200mm with a height of 250mm.
  • Balls dropped in the ball dropping zone rolls on a ramp and falls in the basket of dimensions 600mm X 200mm which is placed in the opponent’s zone.
  • The robots has to pass through a rectangular frame of dimensions 350mm X 350mm in order to reach inner zone.
  • Ball diameter is 68mm and weighs approximately 120 grams.


Procedure:

  • The game is one on one match in which one team competes with the other team.
  • The game lasts for 6 minutes.
  • One extra minute shall be provided for setting up the robots before the start of the match.
  • Only one member of each team is allowed to enter the arena. Only one team member is allowed to control the bot.
  • The robots are placed in their respective starting zones before the match starts.


Tasks of the Robot:

  • As soon as the match begins, the robot has to descend down the ramp and stand on top of two rails. The task will be completed only if the robot stands successfully on top of the rails without falling down. Or the robots are allowed to start from the end of the ramp with a penalty of 1 minute until which the robot has to stand idle.
  • The robot has to pick up one ball from the outer ball zone and place it in the pass zone so that the gate opens.
  • Now the robot has to pick up balls from the outer ball zone and place it on top of the ball dropping zone.
  • After a minimum of 4 balls has been successfully dropped in the ball dropping zone, the robot can pick up balls from either outer ball zone or inner ball zone or can also steal balls from your opponent’s basket. Each carry different points.
  • There will be different colored balls in the inner zone. The outer layer of 12 balls are of green color. Inner 4 balls are of red color. Each carry different points.
  • A ball dropped outside the ball zone by the robot cannot be picked again but will be placed back in its initial position after which it can be picked up.


Scoring:

  • Getting down the ramp successfully awards 2 points.
  • For every ball picked up from the outer ball zone and placed successfully in the ball dropping zone, 2 points will be awarded.
  • For every green ball picked up from the inner ball zone and placed successfully in the ball dropping zone, 3 points will be awarded.
  • For every red ball picked up from the inner ball zone and placed successfully in the ball dropping zone, 6 points will be awarded.
  • For every steal from your opponent’s basket and placed successfully in the ball dropping zone, irrespective of the ball color, 2 points will be awarded.
  • After the end of 6 minutes, each ball in your basket contributes 2 points.


Penalties:

  • For intentionally pulling down the ball dropping zone, 10 points will be deducted.
  • If the robot goes to the opponent’s arena, 5 points will be deducted.
  • If the operator intentionally disturbs the balls with the help of wires, 10 points will be deducted.
  • Damage to arena is unacceptable and leads to disqualification.
  • After the start of the match, if the operator requires to touch the bot (allowed only in case of technical issues) 5 points will be deducted and the robot can resume the match from the top of the ramp. Note: Timer will not be stopped during this time.
